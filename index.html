The Plan, Reordered

The old plan left day three rushed — checking out of the desert, driving back, and barely any time before a 3 PM departure. Flipping the last two nights fixes that: sleep in the desert first, sleep in the city last, and let the final morning actually be relaxed.

11 July Self-Drive

Swift Dzire, self-driven, ₹2,500 for 12 hours. Covers Kuldhara, Khaba Fort, Sam Dunes, and the desert camp — no waiting on a driver, real air conditioning for the drive out.

Fuel: ~6L for 110 km ≈ ₹600–700

12 Eve – 13 Morning 3 Activas

Rented after checking into Hotel Tokyo Palace, kept until the morning of the 13th. Best for the fort's narrow lanes, Gadisar Lake, and the markets — easy parking, fast, no negotiating fares.

Rental ≈ ₹1,800–2,400 · Petrol ≈ ₹150–250

Considered & Skipped

3 Activas for the whole trip — too tiring in 42–45°C heat over the long desert stretch, no luggage space, one rider always alone.

Autos for the city leg — workable (~₹1,200–2,000 for two autos) but slower, with waiting and negotiating built in.

Good to Know

Parking

Not a problem. Park just outside Jaisalmer Fort (vehicles aren't allowed deep inside), Patwon Ki Haveli, and Gadisar Lake, then walk a few minutes in.

Heat

Expect 42–45°C. It's the main reason the long desert leg (11 July) is a self-driven car, not scooters.

Wine Shops

If you're buying alcohol, licensed outlets include the Ganganagar Rajasthan State Liquor Shop and the English Wine & Beer Shop near Hanuman Circle. Typically open 10 AM–10 PM, though hours can shift with local rules and holidays.

Vehicle Handover

Swift Dzire returned on the morning of 12 July once you're back in the city. The 3 Activas are picked up that afternoon and returned before 2:30 PM on the 13th.
